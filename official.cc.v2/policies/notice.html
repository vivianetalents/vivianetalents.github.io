Candidate Privacy Notice

Last Updated: 04 October 2025

This Candidate Privacy Notice supplements our general Privacy Policy and explains how Viviane Talents ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects personal data relating specifically to recruitment candidates ("you").

1. What This Notice Covers

Applies to individuals we consider, source, assess, or present for client opportunities—whether you applied directly, were referred, or were identified through public professional sources.

2. Personal Data We Process About Candidates

  • Core Identity: Name, preferred name, professional profile links, location (city/country).
  • Contact: Email, phone, Telegram/handle, preferred channel, time zone.
  • Career Background: CV/resume content, roles held, achievements, tenure, stack/skills, certifications, education, leadership scope.
  • Position Fit: Compensation expectations, desired role type, seniority, availability/notice period, remote/on‑site preference, relocation interest.
  • Screening Notes: Interview summaries, qualification notes, cultural/value alignment observations (subjectively framed), language proficiency, motivation factors.
  • Process Data: Stage progression, feedback outcomes, offer status, acceptance/decline reasons (if shared).
  • Communications: Emails, messages, scheduling details, clarifications you voluntarily provide.
  • Optional / Sensitive (Only If Voluntarily Provided): Diversity indicators, disability accommodation needs, immigration/work authorization clarifications. We minimize and limit use strictly to legitimate recruitment assessment or legal obligations.

Please avoid sending unnecessary sensitive data. If essential, you acknowledge we may process it strictly to evaluate suitability or comply with law.

3. Sources of Candidate Data

  • You (direct submissions, interviews, messaging).
  • Public professional platforms (LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio sites, conference speaker lists) where lawful.
  • Referrals (friends, colleagues, prior managers) who confirm they have authority to share.
  • Clients (role requirements, feedback on your candidacy).
  • Internal research and talent mapping.

4. Purposes of Processing

  • Assess suitability for active client roles or mapped talent pipelines.
  • Match present or future opportunities based on skill and career trajectory.
  • Facilitate interviews, scheduling, and feedback exchange.
  • Maintain structured search records and performance metrics (aggregated where feasible).
  • Refine sourcing strategy and calibration against client requirements.
  • Comply with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.
  • With consent (where required): retain profile for future roles, send curated opportunities.

5. Legal Bases (EEA / UK / Similar Jurisdictions)

  • Legitimate Interests: Recruitment facilitation for you and clients; relationship management; business operations; network integrity (balanced against your rights).
  • Pre‑Contractual Steps: Actions at your request prior to potential engagement with a client.
  • Consent: Future opportunity retention; optional communications; any sensitive data you voluntarily provide (where required).
  • Legal Obligations: Record-keeping, regulatory inquiries, dispute handling.

6. Who Receives Candidate Data

  • Clients: Only after qualification and alignment; we may summarize strengths, role fit, and contextual notes.
  • Service Providers: Secure hosting, communications, scheduling, storage platforms under confidentiality and data protection agreements.
  • Advisors: Legal/compliance consultants if necessary.
  • Regulators / Legal Requests: Where required by law or to protect rights and safety.
  • Aggregated Insights: Non-identifiable analytics (conversion funnels, skill trends).

We do not sell candidate personal data.

7. International Transfers

Data may be stored or processed in jurisdictions with different protection standards. Where mandated, safeguards (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses, contractual assurances) are applied. By continuing, you acknowledge such transfers subject to applicable law.

8. Retention

We retain candidate data while (a) an active process is ongoing; (b) there is a reasonable prospect of relevant future opportunities; or (c) necessary for legal or operational requirements. Typical review cycles occur every 18–24 months to identify profiles for archival or deletion. You may request earlier deletion unless overriding legitimate grounds or legal obligations apply.

9. Your Rights

Subject to jurisdiction: access; rectification; erasure; restriction; objection (especially to legitimate interest–based processing); portability; withdrawal of consent; complaint to a supervisory authority. See contact section to exercise these rights—verification may be required.

10. Automated Decision-Making

We do not use solely automated decision-making producing legal or similarly significant effects. Limited ranking or filtering tools may assist preliminary triage, always followed by human review.

11. Security Measures

Least‑privilege access, encrypted transit, structured note hygiene, internal confidentiality guidelines, and periodic review. No system is infallible—avoid transmitting excessive sensitive personal data.

12. Referrals

If you refer someone, share only professional contact details and confirm the individual would reasonably expect contact about relevant opportunities. We will provide this Notice to referred candidates at or before first meaningful engagement.

13. Withdrawing from Consideration

You may request: (a) pause; (b) exclusion from a specific client; (c) full removal (subject to legal retention). We log suppression flags to respect future preferences.

14. Updates to This Notice

We may revise this Notice to reflect changes in sourcing practices, tooling, or regulation. The “Last Updated” date changes accordingly. Substantive changes may be highlighted in our general Privacy Policy or during candidate communication.
